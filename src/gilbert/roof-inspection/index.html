The checklist

What I check when I'm on your roof

This isn't a glance from the driveway. I get on the roof and work through the same checklist on every Gilbert home: the condition of the tiles or shingles, the underlayment beneath them, the flashing in valleys and around chimneys, every penetration from vents to skylights to solar mounts, and the fascia.

On tile roofs, the underlayment is where I spend my time, because it's the layer doing the real waterproofing work and the one Arizona heat destroys first. Whether it's holding up or headed toward a tile underlayment replacement is the single most useful thing a Gilbert homeowner can know.

You get photos, a plain-English explanation, and one of three verdicts: it's fine, it needs a repair, or it's time to plan a replacement. Then the decision is yours, on your timeline.

There isn't one. No cost, no obligation, and nobody calls to pressure you afterward. I'm betting that when your roof does eventually need work, you'll remember the roofer who told you the truth for free. That bet is how 90% of my work ends up coming from referrals.

What gets checked on a Gilbert roof inspection?

The tile or shingle surface, the underlayment's condition, flashing at valleys and chimneys, every penetration (vents, skylights, solar mounts), and the fascia boards. You get photos of anything worth seeing and a plain answer about what, if anything, to do.

How old should my roof be before I bother inspecting it?

If it's 15 years or older, it's time, because that's when East Valley underlayment starts giving out. Younger roofs are worth a look after any big monsoon or haboob, before you sell, or any time you spot a cracked tile or a stain on a ceiling.

Will you just tell me I need a new roof like everyone else?

No. I've told plenty of homeowners their roof was fine, including one who was sure he had a problem and just needed a single loose tile reset. If your roof has years left, I'll say so, tell you what to watch, and head out.
