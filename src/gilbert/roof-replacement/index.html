The Gilbert reality

Half of Gilbert doesn't need new tile. It needs new felt.

Before you pay for a "full roof replacement," hear me out. On most Gilbert tile roofs, the tile itself has decades left. What dies at 15 to 20 years is the underlayment, the felt layer that actually keeps water out of your house.

So the smart version of a replacement here is often a tile underlayment replacement: we lift your tiles, install new underlayment, and relay the very same tiles. You get what matters (new waterproofing) without paying for what you don't need (tile you already own). One customer, Lowell, collected 12 estimates before landing on us for exactly this job.

And when a roof genuinely needs everything replaced, I'll say so plainly and show you why. Either way you're choosing from real underlayment samples at different price and quality points, not a line item on a quote.

Worth knowing before you bid it out

How long will my Gilbert roof replacement take?

Most Gilbert homes wrap up in 4 to 5 days, depending on the roof's size and complexity. You'll know the schedule before we start, and my crew cleans the site at the end of every day, so you're never living in a construction zone.

Do I have to buy new tiles when I replace my roof?

Often, no. If your existing tiles are in good condition, we lift them, install new underlayment, and relay the same tiles. You pay for the waterproofing your roof actually needs instead of tile you already own. It's one of the most common jobs we do in Gilbert.

When is the best time of year to replace a roof in Gilbert?

We replace roofs year-round. Demand spikes right before and during monsoon season, so if you know the roof is due, scheduling in the cooler months usually means more flexibility. Either way, don't let a known problem ride through another storm season.

What does a roof replacement cost in Gilbert, AZ?
