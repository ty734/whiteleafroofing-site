Is it time?

When an Apache Junction roof is done

Apache Junction gets the same punishment Gold Canyon does: full sun off the open desert, monsoon wind with nothing in its way, haboob dust in every seam. The difference is what that punishment lands on. Out here it lands on roofs from several different decades, and every era fails on its own schedule.

On tile, the roof can look perfect from the driveway while the underlayment underneath is quietly done; the tile is armor, the felt is the actual waterproofing. Shingle from the 80s and 90s tells you more openly, with curling, cracking and bald spots. And a flat or foam section on an older home fails quietly too, with ponding water and a coating that's chalked out in the sun.

The free inspection settles it. I drive out, walk the roof myself, show you photos of what I find, and tell you honestly whether it's a repair, a replacement, or nothing at all yet.

Do you really come out to Apache Junction?

Yes. I drive out personally for every Apache Junction inspection and bid, free. If you want a ballpark first, call or text me a few photos and I'll give you one, then I walk the roof myself and go over what I find with you face to face.

Can you reuse my existing roof tiles?

Very often, yes. If your tiles are healthy, we lift them, replace the cooked underlayment beneath, and relay the same tiles. In Arizona the felt fails long before the tile does, so a re-felt and relay gives you a watertight roof without paying for tile you already own.

My home has a flat or foam section. Can you replace that too?

Yes. Plenty of Apache Junction homes from the 1970s through the 90s carry a flat or foam section alongside the pitched roof, and I bid the whole roof as one job. Sometimes the flat section needs a recoat rather than a tear-off, and if that's the honest answer, that's the bid you'll get.

Yes, third-party financing is available if you'd rather spread the cost out. I'll go over the options with you at the estimate with zero pressure either way.
