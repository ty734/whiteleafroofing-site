Do you actually need one?

Signs your roof is done, not just tired

Plenty of roofs that "look bad" just need a repair, and I'll tell you when that's the case. But some signs point to a roof at the end of its life: cracked or missing tiles and shingles across the whole field, water stains on ceilings or walls, a sagging roofline, mold or mildew in the attic, and leaks that come back after every monsoon.

The rule of thumb I give homeowners: if repairs are heading past about $4,000, a replacement usually makes more sense. And on tile roofs, the tiles themselves are often fine at 15 to 20 years while the underlayment beneath them is finished. In that case you may only need tile underlayment replacement, which costs far less than a full new roof.

Even if everything looks fine from the ground, hidden damage can be working on your decking. That's what the free inspection is for. I walk the roof myself and give you a straight answer, and sometimes that answer is "you're fine for a few more years."

Your material options

Four ways to roof an Arizona home

/ 01

Tile Roofs

The East Valley standard, and for good reason. Concrete and clay tile handles extreme heat and monsoon rain, and the tiles themselves often last 50 years or more. The underlayment beneath is the part that wears out, so if your tiles are healthy we can lift, re-felt and relay them instead of replacing everything.

Most popular · Tiles often reusable Tile underlayment replacement
/ 02

Shingle Roofs

The most affordable path to a new roof up front. Modern architectural shingles are a big step up from the older 3-tab style, but Arizona UV is hard on any shingle, so plan on a 15 to 20 year service life. A good choice for standard residential homes when budget leads the decision.

Lower upfront cost Get a shingle bid
/ 03

Foam (SPF) Roofs

Spray polyurethane foam is the go-to for Arizona flat and low-slope roofs. Seamless, insulating, and reflective when coated properly. The catch is the coating: foam lives or dies by its recoat schedule, and I'll give you the honest version of what that maintenance looks like.

Flat roof specialist work Foam roofing explained
/ 04

Metal Roofs

Long-lived, fire resistant, and increasingly popular for both modern and ranch-style Arizona homes. Metal sheds heat well and stands up to wind, and it pairs nicely with solar. Higher upfront cost than shingle, but it's a roof you likely won't do twice.

The part nobody shows you

You pick the underlayment, with samples in hand

Underlayment is the waterproof layer under your tile or shingle, and it's the single biggest quality decision in a replacement. Most companies quote one product and never show it to you. I bring physical samples of several underlayment options to the estimate so you can feel the difference and choose the price and quality point that fits your plans for the house.

One of my customers, Bill Durham, put it this way after replacing his 20-year-old roof: "He offered us several underlayment options, as well as samples for us to compare. I liked that I was able to choose the product and price point that suited our needs... he was here everyday."

Manufacturer warranties depend on the material you choose, and I'll walk you through the real documentation for each option. For what a full project costs, see the Arizona roof replacement cost guide.

Replacement questions

How long does a roof replacement take?

Most residential replacements are done in 2 to 4 days depending on the size and complexity of the roof. I give you a real schedule up front and my crew keeps to it. Michelle Ruyle's crew actually started early and finished early.

How much does a roof replacement cost in Arizona?

It depends on roof size, material and the underlayment you choose, which is why every job gets a free individual bid. I'm almost always able to beat the big companies' bids because there's no sales team baked into my price. The Arizona cost guide has real numbers, and third-party financing is available.

Can you reuse my existing roof tiles?

In many cases, yes. If the tiles are in good shape we lift them, replace the failed underlayment beneath, and relay the same tiles. It costs far less than a full replacement. Details on the tile underlayment replacement page.

What are the signs I need a roof replacement?

Cracked or missing tiles and shingles across the field, water stains on ceilings or walls, a sagging roofline, mold or mildew in the attic, and leaks after monsoon rains. If repair costs climb past about $4,000, replacement usually makes more sense. If you're on the fence, start with a repair inspection.

What about surprise costs after the job starts?

The bid is the bid. When we found unexpected wood damage on a Chandler replacement, I fixed it at no extra cost. No nickel and diming is how I run the company.

Do new roofs come with a warranty?

Manufacturer warranties depend on the material you choose. I'll walk you through the real options and documentation for each product during the estimate, so you know exactly what you're getting before you sign anything.
