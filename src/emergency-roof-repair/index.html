Monsoons, haboobs and hail

What Arizona storms do to a roof

Every summer, roughly June through September, monsoon season throws high winds, driving rain, hail and haboobs at East Valley roofs. Strong winds lift and crack tiles, tear shingles and flashing, and heavy rain finds every weak spot in aging underlayment. Haboob dust and debris clog gutters and valleys so the next storm's water has nowhere to go.

The tricky part: storm damage often isn't visible from the ground, and it may not leak until the next rain. A cracked tile or lifted flashing can sit quietly for weeks, letting small amounts of water weaken the decking underneath. By the time a stain shows on the ceiling, the damage has been working for a while.

That's why the free post-storm inspection matters. I do a full walk-around, check the field, flashing, valleys and gutters, and give you an honest damage report. If your roof came through fine, I'll tell you that and you can stop worrying. If it didn't, we fix it fast, and temporary tarping keeps water out until permanent repairs happen.

Active leaks and storm damage go to the front of the line, often same or next day. If the roof is too wet to walk safely, I assess what I can immediately and return the moment it dries, before the next round of rain when possible. That's exactly how Matthew Crandle's leak got handled.

Can a haboob really damage a roof?

Yes. Haboob winds lift and crack tiles, tear at shingles and flashing, and drive dust and debris into every gap. The damage often isn't visible from the ground and doesn't leak until the next rain, which is why a free walk-around inspection after a big storm is worth doing.

Will my insurance cover storm damage roof repairs?

In many cases, yes. I document the damage with photos and a detailed report, and help you communicate with your insurance company. If the damage doesn't warrant a claim, I'll tell you that too. Details on the insurance claims page.

Can you protect my roof before permanent repairs?

Yes. When a roof needs time before permanent repair, temporary protection like tarping keeps water out so the damage doesn't spread while the real fix gets scheduled.
