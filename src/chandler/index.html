Local knowledge

Chandler roofs, street by street

Most of Chandler was built between the 1980s and the 2000s, which means a lot of concrete tile roofs in neighborhoods like Ocotillo, Fulton Ranch, Clemente Ranch, Pecos Ranch, Sun Groves and Cooper Commons are sitting on original underlayment that's past its service life. The tiles look fine from the street. The felt underneath is what fails.

That's why so much of my Chandler work is tile underlayment replacement: we lift your existing tiles, replace the failed underlayment, and relay the same tiles. You get decades more roof life without paying for a full new roof.

Add Chandler's summer UV, monsoon wind and the occasional haboob, and the honest move is simple: get the roof looked at before the next storm season, not after. The inspection is free and I do it myself.
