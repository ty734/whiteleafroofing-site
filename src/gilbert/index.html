Local knowledge

Why Gilbert roofs are all aging at once

Gilbert grew fast. Whole square miles of it, Val Vista Lakes, Morrison Ranch, Power Ranch, Agritopia, Seville, Lyon's Gate, The Islands, went up in the 1990s and 2000s, almost all of it under concrete tile. The tile is still fine. The felt underlayment beneath it lasts 15 to 20 years in Arizona heat, and the math on that is catching up with half the town at the same time.

So a huge share of my Gilbert work is tile underlayment replacement: lift your tiles, replace the failed felt, relay the same tiles. Decades more roof life, at a fraction of what a full new roof costs. One Gilbert-area customer got 12 estimates before picking us for exactly that job.

My advice is the same on every street from the Heritage District to Power Ranch: get the roof looked at before monsoon season instead of after. The inspection is free and I do it myself.
