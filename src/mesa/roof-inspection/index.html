What I look at

A real inspection, not a sales visit

When I walk a Mesa roof, I'm checking five things: the tiles or shingles themselves, the condition of the underlayment, the flashing at chimneys and valleys, every penetration (vents, skylights, satellite mounts), and the fascia.

On this side of the Valley the underlayment is the one that matters most. So much of East Mesa was built in the 1990s and 2000s that whole neighborhoods are hitting the felt's failure window at the same time. From the street those tile roofs look perfect. Up close, I can tell you whether yours has years left or whether it's time to plan for a tile underlayment replacement.

You get photos of what I find and a plain-English verdict: leave it alone, repair it, or start thinking about replacement. Whatever the roof actually needs, that's what I'll say.

Mesa inspection questions

Fair things to ask first

Is the roof inspection in Mesa really free?

Yes. It costs nothing, there's no obligation, and no salesman follows up to hound you. I drive over from Chandler, walk the roof myself, and talk you through what I find. That's the whole deal.

Do you do pre-purchase roof inspections in Mesa?

Yes. If you're buying in Las Sendas, Eastmark, Mountain Bridge or anywhere else in Mesa, I'll walk the roof before you close and tell you what it needs now, what it will need later, and roughly what that costs. It's the same free inspection, just on a buyer's timeline.

Can I get a roof certification for a home sale?

If a buyer, seller or lender needs the roof's condition in writing, I'll inspect it and put what I find on paper, photos included. If the roof needs work before it can pass muster, I'll bid that honestly. Most repairs run $500 to $3,500.

When should I get my Mesa roof inspected?

Before monsoon season, after any big storm or haboob, before buying or selling a home, and any time a tile roof from the 1990s or 2000s has never had its underlayment checked. Catching a worn spot early is a repair. Catching it late is drywall, insulation and a bigger bill.

Do I need to be home for the inspection?

It helps but it's not required. If you're home I'll walk you through everything face to face. If not, I'll send photos and give you the full rundown by phone or text, whichever you prefer. Mesa is a short drive from my shop in Chandler, so scheduling is usually quick.
