Local knowledge

Roofs from every era, one exposure

Apache Junction sits at the foot of the Superstition Mountains, off US 60 where the Valley runs out and the open desert takes over. The exposure out here is the same one Gold Canyon gets: relentless sun, monsoon wind crossing open desert with nothing to slow it, and haboob dust working into every seam.

What makes Apache Junction different is the housing itself. This town wasn't built in one decade. Established neighborhoods from the 1970s through the 90s sit alongside newer builds, so on one street I'll see concrete tile, aged shingle, and flat or foam sections on the older homes. Each of those ages differently under the same sun, and each one gets repaired or replaced differently.

On the tile roofs, the tiles usually outlive the felt beneath them, so a lot of my Apache Junction work is tile underlayment replacement: lift your existing tiles, replace the failed felt, relay the same tiles. On the older shingle and flat roofs, honest work starts with knowing what era of material I'm standing on.

And about the distance: Apache Junction is a longer drive from Chandler, and I come out personally anyway. Ballpark by phone or text if you want one first, then I walk the roof myself and go over what I find with you face to face. Same free inspection I'd give a neighbor down the street.
