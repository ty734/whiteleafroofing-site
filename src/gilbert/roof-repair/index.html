What breaks on Gilbert roofs

/ 01

Monsoon wind damage

Gilbert catches the brunt of summer storms rolling up from the southeast valley. Lifted tiles, torn felt, and debris strikes, often with no leak until the next rain finds the gap.

Free walk-around after storms
/ 02

Slipped & cracked tiles

One out-of-place tile is a cheap fix and a big deal: it exposes the felt below to sun and rain. I reset or match tiles and check the surrounding field while I'm up there.

Small fix, big protection
/ 03

Underlayment leaks

The classic Gilbert problem. Homes in Val Vista Lakes, Power Ranch and Seville are hitting the age where the felt under the tile gives out. Small sections can be repaired on their own.

Repair the patch, keep the tiles
/ 04

Flashing & vent failures

Valleys, chimneys, skylights and vent stacks cause more leaks than the roof surface itself. Old sealant and tired metal get replaced so water stays on the outside.

Where most leaks really start
/ 05

Heat-beaten materials

A hundred-plus days over 100 degrees cooks sealants, shingles and felt. I'll tell you what's aged out, what's still serviceable, and what to budget for down the road.

Honest condition report
/ 06

Active leaks

Leaking right now? Call me, not a call center. I'll get eyes on it fast, stop the water, and do the permanent fix as soon as the roof is dry enough to walk safely.

How much does roof repair cost in Gilbert, AZ?

Most Gilbert repairs land between $500 and $3,500. The exact number depends on what failed and how far the water got, so I inspect first, free, then bid the job individually. Once a repair pushes past about $3,500, a replacement usually makes more sense, and I'll be upfront about that.

Do you handle emergency roof repairs in Gilbert?

Yes. Active leaks and fresh storm damage jump the line. Our shop is on Gilbert Rd at the Chandler border, so getting to any Gilbert neighborhood quickly is easy, often the same or next day.

My tile roof is leaking. Do I need a whole new roof?

Usually not. Most Gilbert tile leaks come from a failed patch of underlayment or a slipped tile, and both can be repaired on their own. Even when the underlayment is done across the whole roof, your tiles can usually be lifted and relaid over new felt, which costs far less than a new roof.

How do I know if monsoon wind damaged my roof?

Often you won't until it rains again, because wind lifts tiles and flashing without leaving an obvious leak. If a storm rolled through your part of Gilbert, take a photo of anything that looks off and text it to me at 480-363-2898, or just have me do a free walk-around.
