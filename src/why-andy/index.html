My story

Raised in the trade

My dad ran a contracting business, and I grew up in it. By the time I was 18 I'd worked nearly every position on his crew: tear-off, hauling, loading, laying underlayment, setting tile. There's no part of a roofing job I haven't done with my own hands.

Spend 25 years in this industry and you see the good and, unfortunately, a lot of the bad. Fly-by-night outfits that aren't properly licensed. Companies that cut corners with cheap materials to protect their overhead. Salesmen who get paid to close, not to tell the truth.

So when I started White Leaf, I decided it would be the opposite of all that. I answer my own phone. I do every inspection and every estimate myself. And I personally oversee every job we take. That's how I make sure the work coming out of my company is work I'd sign my name to, because I do.

How I run the company

The four standards

You deal with the owner

Call or text 480-363-2898 and you get me, not a call center. I do every inspection and every estimate personally, free. If I'm on a roof, I call you back.

Honest repair or replace

I don't employ commissioned salesmen, so nobody profits by overselling you. Most repairs run $500 to $3,500, and if a repair is all you need, that's all I'll recommend. Here's how I decide.

Materials I'd use on my own home

I only put materials on your house that I'd put on mine, installed by a crew I hand-picked from the best people I've worked with in 25 years.

No surprises

We start on time, clean up every single day, and finish when promised. No surprise costs after the job starts. If something pops up, we fix it. Period.
