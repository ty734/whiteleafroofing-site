What the free inspection is

I walk the roof. All of it.

A real roof inspection isn't a guy glancing at your house from the driveway. When I inspect a roof, I get up on it and check everything that actually fails on Arizona homes:

  • 01Tiles. Cracked, slipped or loose tiles, and whether your existing tile can be reused if the roof ever needs work.
  • 02Underlayment. I pull tiles in several spots to see the felt underneath. This is what actually decides repair vs. replace on most East Valley roofs.
  • 03Flashing and penetrations. Valleys, vents, skylights and anywhere a pipe comes through the roof. Most leaks start here.
  • 04Fascia and eaves. Dry rot and water damage at the edges, the early warning signs most homeowners never see.

Then I come down and explain what I found in plain English. No jargon, no scare tactics, no invoice. If it needs work, you'll get a clear bid with options. If it doesn't, I'll tell you that too.

Good times to get one

When a free roof inspection makes sense

/ 01

Your roof is 15+ years old

Tile roofs from the 1990s and 2000s are hitting the age where the underlayment fails even though the tiles look fine from the street. An inspection tells you how much life is really left.

The most common reason people call
/ 02

Before monsoon season

The honest move is to get the roof looked at before the storms, not after the ceiling stains show up. Small fixes now beat emergency calls in July.

Spring is the smart window
/ 03

After a storm or haboob

Wind lifts tiles and drives debris into valleys and flashing. I'll do a full walk-around, give you an honest damage report, and help with an insurance claim if the damage warrants one.

Fast response after storms
/ 04

Before selling your home

Know what the buyer's inspector will find before they find it. You can fix small issues on your terms instead of negotiating repairs under deadline pressure.

No surprises at closing
/ 05

Before adding solar

Panels can outlive the underlayment beneath them, and removing them later to fix the roof is expensive. Make sure the roof is solid before anything gets bolted to it.

Check the roof first, then the panels

You're collecting bids
