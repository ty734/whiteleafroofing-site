Local knowledge

Tile roofs against the Superstitions

Gold Canyon is beautiful precisely because it's exposed: open desert, big sky, and the Superstitions right there. Your roof pays for that view. The sun out here is relentless, and monsoon wind crosses the foothills with almost nothing to slow it down.

Most homes in Gold Canyon and Peralta Trails wear concrete tile, and tile is the right material for this exposure. But the tiles are only half the roof. The underlayment beneath them is what actually keeps water out, and Arizona heat cooks it on a schedule regardless of how good the tiles still look from the driveway.

That's why so much of my Gold Canyon work is tile underlayment replacement: lift your existing tiles, replace the failed felt, relay the same tiles. Decades more roof life without paying for tile you don't need.

And on the question of distance: it doesn't change a thing. Preliminary ballpark by phone or text, then I come out personally for the inspection and go over what I find with you face to face. Materials, pricing, financing options if you want them, and zero pressure.
